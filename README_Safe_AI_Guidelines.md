## Overview

A compliance-grounded guide for safe, responsible, and legally aware use of Microsoft 365 Copilot in professional environments. Updated for Q1 2026 to include the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), EU AI Act implementation timeline, current Microsoft Purview controls, and the NIST AI RMF vs ISO 42001 framework comparison.

Produced as a portfolio artifact for Texas DIR Req 5944 and as a reusable LachesisAI deliverable for enterprise Copilot governance programs.

## Key Updates from v1 (March 2026)

- **TDPSA added**: Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (HB 4, Ch. 541 Texas B&C Code); effective July 1, 2024; $7,500/violation enforcement
- **EU AI Act timeline corrected**: Aug 2024 in force; Feb 2025 prohibited practices; Aug 2, 2026 full enforcement — deployer obligations detailed
- **Microsoft Purview expanded**: DSPM for AI, sensitivity label inheritance, DLP, audit logs, 30-day deletion commitment
- **NIST AI RMF vs ISO 42001 comparison table** added with actionability assessment for mid-size enterprises
- **NERC CIP section** added — relevant for electric utility environments (no specific AI controls exist; existing CIP-005/007/010/011/013 apply)
- **Prohibited data warning box** added with explicit categories
- Removed outdated GDPR-only framing; Texas-specific regulatory context added throughout
